The Ancient Aztec God of lightning

Xolotl's sacrafice

HEADLINE

It is believed in mexican mythology that the axolotl used to be a god. He's said to have turned himself into a type of salamander to avoid a self sacrafice. A sacrafice was expected to be made by the gods who created the fifth sun. It was agreeed that they would do so in order to nourish the sun that was stagnant in it's position in the sky.

The myth surrounding the unique Mexican axolotl involves Xolotl, an Aztec god determined to escape his impending sacrifice.
